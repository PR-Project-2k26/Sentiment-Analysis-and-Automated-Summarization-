The crux of the matter as held out by a host was the concept of dimensions in the manner it is generally understood and taught. A thing that does not possesses dimension does not exist. For example, a mathematical line of zero thickness or a mathematical plane does not exist. There are only abstractions. Similarly, a cube which is three dimensional object cannot exist only on the strength of its length, breadth and thickness. This claim of a host made Philby's hair stand on its end. There I object, he said immediately. In response, a host possessed a cushion. Can a cube that does not last for any time at all have a real existence? This set everyone thinking. A host continued to explain. Any real body must have extension in four directions. It must have length, breadth, thickness and duration. He stated that there are really four dimensions, three which are generally referred to as three planes of space and a fourth of time. In reality, they are all alike in nature and there is no real distinction between the former three and the fourth. In conventional mathematics, the three dimensions of length, breadth and thickness are definable by the reference to their three planes, each at right angles to others. But why not another directions at right angles to the other three and have a four dimensional symmetry on a flat surface which has only two dimensions. One can draw the figure of three dimensional solid. This is achieved by creating the perspective of three dimensions of the objective. Similarly, a three dimensional model is capable of the perspective of four dimensions. He went on to state that time is only kind of space. In a weather chart, the movement of the barometer is represented by tracing a line. It shows the highs through the days and dips during the nights. The mercury trace the progress line not in conventional three dimensions, but in time line, the time dimension.
