* **Main Topic**: The Future of Jobs in the Age of Artificial Intelligence
* **Key Points**:
  * AI is transforming jobs, not replacing them
  * New roles are being created, such as AI engineers and automation specialists
  * Students should focus on building real skills, not just studying for marks
  * Companies value what you can do, not just your degree
  * Learning AI doesn't require expert coding skills from the start
  * Practical skills, such as problem-solving and communication, are becoming increasingly important
* **Important Details**:
  * Students who start early, experimenting with tools and building projects, have a huge advantage
  * Creating a strong portfolio is more valuable than just having good marks
  * Simple tools, such as no-code platforms, can be used to start learning AI
  * Internships and volunteering experiences provide valuable real-world experience
  * Students should focus on solving problems they're interested in, rather than just following trends
* **Final Insight**: Instead of fearing AI, students should be flexible, curious, and willing to learn continuously, focusing on building skills, experimenting, and taking small steps consistently to stay ahead in the future job market.
